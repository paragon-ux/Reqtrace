The documents follow a top-down progression from "why" to "how": BRD → ARD → DRD → TRD. They flow from high-level business objectives to the concrete, technical specifications executed by developers.

1. BRD (Business Requirements Document): Defines *why* the project is being done and its high-level business goals, scope, and objectives.  
2. ARD (Architecture Requirements Document): Details the overarching system structure, components, and topology needed to support the BRD.  
3. DRD (Data Requirements Document): Specifies the data models, storage schemas, and data flows within that architecture.  
4. TRD (Technical Requirements Document): The final step, breaking down the exact hardware, software, and engineering requirements needed for implementation.
